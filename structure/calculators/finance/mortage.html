A mortgage calculator is a tool used to determine the monthly payment and overall cost of a mortgage loan. A mortgage loan is a type of loan used to finance the purchase of a property, typically a home. The loan is paid back over a period of time, usually several decades, with interest added on top of the principal.

The formula used by a mortgage calculator typically includes several variables, including the amount of the loan, the interest rate, the term of the loan, and any additional costs, such as property taxes and insurance.

Formula: M = P * (r(1 + r)^n)/((1 + r)^n - 1)

Where:

M = monthly payment amount

P = principal amount of the loan

r = monthly interest rate (as a decimal)

n = total number of payments

Note: You'll need to convert the annual interest rate to a monthly interest rate and the loan term from years to months to use this formula.

Using a mortgage calculator, you can input these variables to determine your monthly payment and the total cost of the loan over the full term of the mortgage. This can help you determine how much you can afford to borrow, how much your monthly payment will be, and the overall cost of the mortgage over the life of the loan.
